# Data Science Job Market & Salary Analysis (2020–2025) | Python

> **Business Question:** What factors most strongly predict salary in data roles — and what does the data tell someone early in their analytics career about where to focus?


##  The Problem

The data science job market grew dramatically between 2020 and 2025, but not evenly. Some roles, locations, and experience levels saw salary growth of 2–3x. Others stagnated. This analysis explores what actually drives salary variation — not assumptions, but patterns in 5 years of real job market data.

The findings are directly relevant to anyone making career decisions in data: which role to target, which market to enter, and how experience level translates to pay.



##  Key Business Findings

**1. Experience level is the single largest salary driver.**
The gap between entry-level and senior roles is not marginal — it is the largest determinant of salary, outpacing both job title and company size. Early investment in building a portfolio and getting real experience has compounding financial returns.

**2. Job title variation explains more than most people expect.**
"Data Analyst" and "Data Scientist" are not interchangeable salary bands. The median salary difference between certain titles exceeds $40,000 USD annually. Role selection matters as much as experience level.

**3. Salary has increased consistently year-over-year from 2020–2025.**
The market did not plateau. Demand for data professionals continued to grow through the period, with median compensation rising each year. This is a sustained trend, not a pandemic-era anomaly.

**4. Company size and location interact significantly.**
Large companies in certain locations pay a substantial premium over similarly-titled roles at smaller companies in the same region. 

**5. Salary distribution has significant outliers.**
The top 10% of earners pull the mean significantly above the median. When evaluating salary benchmarks, the median is more representative than the mean for most job seekers.



## What This Means 

For someone early in their data career, this analysis points to three clear actions:

1. **Prioritise getting experience over accumulating certificates.** The data shows experience level is the dominant salary variable. Time spent building portfolio projects and getting real work experience returns more than additional credentials.

2. **Be deliberate about role titles.** "Data Analyst" and "Business Intelligence Analyst" at the same company in the same city can have meaningfully different salary trajectories. Understanding the title landscape before applying is worth the research.

3. **Target larger companies for early career, if salary growth is the goal.** The data shows a consistent size premium. Getting your first role at a larger organisation — even at a lower starting title — accelerates the overall compensation curve.
